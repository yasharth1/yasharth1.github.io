About: Neeraj Chopra (born 24 December 1997) is an Indian javelin thrower, and a
Junior Commissioned Officer (JCO) in the Indian Army. He is the first track and
field athlete to win a gold medal for India at the Olympics. In the 2016 IAAF
World U20 Championships, Chopra achieved a world under-20
record of 86.48 m. He participated in the 2018 Commonwealth Games and

In his debut at 2020 Summer Olympics, Chopra won the gold medal on
7 August 2021 with a throw of 87.58m in his second attempt. As of 2021, he is one of

being Abhinav Bindra), as well as the youngest-ever Indian gold medalist in
an individual event and the only one to have won
gold in his Olympic debut.
